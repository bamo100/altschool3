Nigerian Jollof Rice

Discover the rich, flavorful world of Nigerian Jollof Rice - a beloved West African dish that combines perfectly seasoned rice with tomatoes, peppers, and aromatic spices. This one-pot wonder is the heart of Nigerian celebrations and a true comfort food that brings families together around the dinner table.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Number of servings: 6-8 people

Difficulty level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 pound chicken, cut into pieces (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 stock cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (optional, for heat)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the base: Heat vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add diced onions and saut√© until translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  2. Build the flavor: Add tomato paste to the onions and fry for 2-3 minutes until it darkens slightly. Then add the blended tomatoes and peppers, cooking for 15-20 minutes until the mixture reduces and the raw taste disappears.
  3. Season the sauce: Add curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, and stock cubes. If using chicken, add it now and cook for 10 minutes until partially cooked through.
  4. Add the rice: Rinse the rice until water runs clear, then add to the pot. Stir gently to coat each grain with the tomato mixture, being careful not to break the rice.
  5. Perfect the cooking: Pour in the stock gradually, add scotch bonnet pepper if desired, and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and simmer for 20-25 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  6. Rest and serve: Remove from heat and let stand for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork. Remove bay leaves and serve hot with your favorite sides.

Tips & Notes

Pro tip: The secret to perfect Jollof rice is achieving the right balance of liquid to rice ratio and allowing the bottom to get slightly crispy for that authentic "party rice" flavor that Nigerians love. Don't stir too much during cooking - let the rice do its magic! For extra richness, you can add a tablespoon of butter at the end of cooking.

Enjoy this taste of West Africa! Traditionally served at celebrations, weddings, and Sunday family dinners, Nigerian Jollof Rice pairs beautifully with fried plantains, coleslaw, or grilled meat.
